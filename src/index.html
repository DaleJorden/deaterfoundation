Deater & Rare Inherited Neuropathy

Hereditary Sensory and Autonomic Neuropathy Type 1 (HSAN1) is a rare genetic disorder that affects the nervous system. This progressive condition impacts sensory and autonomic nerves, leading to loss of sensation, particularly in the feet and hands.

The Deater Foundation was established to fund critical research aimed at understanding the genetic mechanisms of HSAN1 and developing potential treatments and cures for this debilitating disease.

The Research

For over 30 years, the Deater Foundation has supported cutting-edge research into HSAN1, funding studies that have led to the identification of the gene causing HSAN1 and advancing our understanding of treatment options.

Our funding has enabled world-class researchers to make breakthrough discoveries in the mechanisms of this disease, bringing us closer to effective treatments and ultimately a cure.

Our Leadership

The Deater Foundation is led by a dedicated board of directors, many of whom have personal connections to HSAN1 through family members affected by the disease.

Our leadership brings together expertise in medicine, research, business, and nonprofit management to advance our mission of finding a cure for HSAN1.
