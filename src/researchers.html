The Researchers

Information is from original pamphlet on the website, produced in 2011, detailing currently supported research on different currently working with HSAN1.

Photo

Robert H. Brown Jr., MD, PhD

Chair, Department of Neurology
University of Massachusetts Chan Medical School

Dr. Brown is director of the Program in Neurofibromatosis and director of the Day Neuromuscular Laboratory. He has made leading discoveries related to various forms of motor neuron disease, including the biology of SOX10, which is also associated with HSAN1. He has served as National Coordinator for ALS clinical trials, and was a key figure when the gene causing HSAN1 was discovered.

Photo

Florian Eichler, MD

Director, MGH Neuroscience Center
Massachusetts General Hospital

Dr. Eichler focuses on neurogenetics, particularly the study of leukodystrophies and peripheral neuropathies. His research laboratory explores sphingolipid metabolism and its neurological implications. He has been instrumental in conducting clinical trials for HSAN1, including the L-serine treatment studies.
